How to Play

Double 12 Express is based on Mexican Train‚Äìstyle play. Build your train by matching ends, manage doubles, and try to empty your hand first.

  1. Setup: Each player draws 15 tiles. The remaining tiles form the boneyard.
  2. Starting (Engines): The first round begins with the double-12 (the Engine) placed to start play. Subsequent rounds begin with double-11, double-10, and so on down to double-blank.
  3. Gameplay: On your turn, play one tile that matches the open end of a train. You may play on your own train or the Express Line. (See ‚ÄúPublic Trains‚Äù below for when you can play on other players‚Äô trains.)
  4. No Move: If you cannot play a matching tile, you must draw one tile from the boneyard. If you can play after drawing, you may play that turn. If you still cannot play, you must pass.
  5. Doubles (Satisfy the Double): Normally you play only one tile per turn. If you play a double, it must be satisfied by immediately playing the next tile onto that double on the same train.
    • If you cannot satisfy the double, you draw one from the boneyard and either satisfy it or pass.
    • If still unsatisfied, the next player must try to satisfy it (draw one if needed), and play or pass.
    • This continues until the double is satisfied, then normal play resumes.
  6. Public Trains: If you pass, your train becomes open (‚Äúpublic‚Äù). Any player may play on a public train, their own train, or the Express Line.
  7. Winning & Scoring: A round ends when a player plays all of their tiles. Other players score the sum of pips on tiles remaining in their hands. After all rounds, the player with the lowest total score wins.
